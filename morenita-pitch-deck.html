The Problem
The communities that
most need good technology
are the last to receive it.
Over 3 billion people globally lack meaningful access to quality education, clean energy, and the tools that build prosperity. The mainstream technology industry builds for the already-connected. The gap compounds with every product cycle.

At the same time, the tools that do exist are fragmented — the platform that teaches doesn't manufacture, the manufacturer doesn't generate its own energy, the energy company doesn't serve the community around it. Nothing closes the loop.

Morenita Collective closes the loop.

The
Digibook
A physical book with 30 paper-like screens powered by a projector in the spine. Your personalized weekly magazine — curated around art, science, nature, and creativity — appears on the pages. Write and sketch with a custom stylus. Everything saves to the Library of Morenita platform automatically.
Solar-powered case. Recycled components. Low-energy architecture. Designed to feel slow and analog while fully connected to a digital world. In a time when everything is designed to move fast, the Digibook is designed to do the opposite.

Library of Morenita
MAXINE
A healthcare recovery companion device that assists nurses and caretakers by streamlining patient tracking, medication administration, and bookkeeping across multiple patients simultaneously.
Nurse burnout is one of the leading crises in healthcare globally. The administrative burden — not the clinical work — is the primary driver. MAXINE removes it. One device. Many patients. Every nurse who uses it gets their time back.
